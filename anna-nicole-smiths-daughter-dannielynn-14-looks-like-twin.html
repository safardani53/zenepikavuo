ANNA Nicole Smith's 14-year-old daughter is the spitting image of the model in a new ABC documentary released 14-years after her mother's death by drug overdose.

The new film Tragic Beauty will debut Friday, February 5th, and will touch on Dannielynn's life without her mom.

A clip of the movie shared on Twitter exposed details that were uncovered regarding Anna's childhood and early life leading up to her devastating death.

In a montage from old footage of the Playboy model paired with a video of Anna's only surviving child, Dannielynn, 14, the resemblance is unmistakable.

The teen shares the same facial structure and even hairstyle that the model sported as a young lady.

When visiting a friend in the preview, Dannielynn hugged the woman as she gushed: "You are your mommy. You look just like her."

In another side by side collage, their nose, eyebrow, and eye shape are identical, as the child grows into an adult that the film claims is "just like her."

Dannielynn's father, Larry Birkhead, narrated the teaser, explaining: "She wanted to be a star, she wanted to be a mother, and Dannielynn is her legacy.

"Dannielynn is a teenager now, and she doesn't know a ton of things about her mom," he admitted.

"As a dad who has a child whose mom passed away, I thought why not go back to Anna's beginnings."

"And to be really honest, I didn't know a whole lot about Anna in her early years," the celebrity photographer shared.

Larry and his daughter went on a "search" to find out more about the blonde icon, including details on her "possessions", her "secrets" and her death.

Anna Nicole Smith rose to fame as a model after dropping out of high school in Texas at age 14.

The actress married her first husband Billy Smith at age 17, with whom she birthed her first child Daniel in 1984.

While working as a stripper in Houston in 1991, Anna met Texas oil tycoon J. Howard Marshall II.

After a series of campaigns, Anna's career took off when she was featured as Playboy's Playmate of the Year in 1993.

The media was entranced with her tumultuous life, though initially she claimed to not find it bothersome.

She once told the Washington Post: "I love the paparazzi. They take pictures, and I just smile away. I've always liked attention. I didn't get very much growing up, and I always wanted to be, you know, noticed."

Anna married J. Howard in 1994, when she was 26 and he was 89.

Their marriage sparked heavy controversy, as many believed the model was using him for his billions.

Just a year later, J. Howard passed away, igniting a court battle over his will which lasted a grueling 11 years.

Anna was given her own reality TV series in 2002, The Anna Nicole Show, which was cut two years later due to the actress's disoriented nature.

The television personality was seeing several men when she gave birth to Dannielynn in 2006, sparking a custody battle that continued well past her death.

Tragically, the model's 20-year-old son died just three days after Dannielynn's birth.

His loss was due to a drug overdose, which reportedly occurred in Anna's maternity hospital room in Nassau, Bahamas.

Anna died on February 8, 2007, five months after the birth of her daughter and passing of her son, and almost 14 years ago to the day.

The mother of two fell unconscious due to a prescription drug overdose at the Seminole Hard Rock Hotel and Casino in Hollywood, Florida, and was unable to be recessitated.
