Fernando Alonso | Professional Career

Fernando Alonso signed with Adrian Campos to compete in the 1999 Spanish Euro Open. He won the title and moved to Formula 3000, ending fourth that season. 

Later, in 2001 Alonso debuted in Formula One with Minardi in the Australian Grand Prix.

In 2002, he signed with Renault as a test driver completing more than 1600 laps of testing. Next season, he was promoted to the race team of Renault. 

Champion

In 2005, he went to win San Marino Grand Prix, British Grand Prix, and Brazil Prix. At the end of the year, he became the youngest  F1 champion with 133 points. 

In 2006, Alonso broke the record for being the first driver to secure 1st/2nd in the first nine races of the campaign.

After moving to Mclaren in 2007, he won his debut race in Malaysia. Likewise, he also secured victories at Monaco and Nurburgring. 

However, in 2020 he signed with Alpine making a comeback from his retirement.

But in his debut race for the team, he was forced to retire due to the entrance of plastic debris in his car’s brake duct.
