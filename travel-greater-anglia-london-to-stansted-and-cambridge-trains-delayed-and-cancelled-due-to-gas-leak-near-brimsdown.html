TRAIN services around London, Hertfordshire, Cambridgeshire and Essex have been delayed or cancelled this morning after a gas leak.

It has left Abellio Greater Anglia’s line between Cheshunt and Tottenham Hale closed following the leak near Brimsdown station.

#Brimsdown - Stansted Express & Cambridge services are running at reduced frequency via Seven Sisters.. RS

— Greater Anglia (@greateranglia) August 16, 2016

Stansted airport and Cambridge services are running at a reduced frequency.

Passengers for Tottenham Hale, Northumberland Park, Angel Road, Ponders End, Brimsdown, Enfield Lock and Waltham Cross can use their tickets on buses, the underground and overground trains.

On social media commuters hit out at the service, calling it "unacceptable".

On Twitter @MattwBrady wrote: "Being delayed on 4 out of 5 commutes is unacceptable, whatever the reason. It costs me over £4000 for this service."

related stories

A statement on Greater Anglia's website said: "Owing to a gas leak near Brimsdown station the line between Cheshunt and Tottenham Hale has been closed.
Train services are being diverted via Seven Sisters and stations between Tottenham Hale and Waltham Cross are not being served. An amended train service will be in place shortly.

"Customers for Tottenham Hale, Northumberland Park, Angel Road, Ponders End, Brimsdown, Enfield Lock and Waltham Cross are advised that TfL Underground, TFL buses and London Overground are accepting Abellio Greater Anglia tickets on any reasonable route.

"Stansted Airport and Cambridge services are running at a reduced frequency diverting via Seven Sisters. Train services to  and from Stratford to Hertford East and Bishops Stortford via Brimsdown have been suspended, customers should travel via Liverpool Street."
