JESSICA Simpson has called out a Vogue writer for claiming her breasts were "on a platter" in a dress she wore to the Met Gala in 2007.

The 39-year-old singer said she was "nauseated" at being "body-shamed for having boobs" in a lengthy caption on Instagram.

Jessica shared an iconic photo from 1957 which shows Sophia Loren casting a sideways glance at Jayne Mansfield's cleavage at a dinner held in the Italian star's honor.

She wrote: "Feeling a little like Jayne Mansfield after reading this (inaccurate!) oral history of the #MetBall where I am body shamed.

"But in all seriousness I have persevered through shaming my own body and internalizing the world’s opinions about it for my entire adult life."

The mother-of-three went on: "To read this much anticipated article about the classiest fashion event there is and have to be shamed by another woman for having boobs in 2020 is nauseating."

Former Vogue creative digital director Sally Singer had shared her memories about the Met Gala in a piece for the magazine.

She had been seated with Jessica and her then-boyfriend, singer John Mayer at the dinner.

She wrote: "One year Jessica Simpson was there with John Mayer. She was wearing Michael Kors and her breasts maybe fell out of her dress on the red carpet... and then at dinner it was suddenly like, whoa, Jessica Simpson's breasts are across from me at the dinner table and they are on a platter and I'm looking at them."

Sally went on: "And John Mayer was putting his hands on them at the dinner table.

"He kind of reached down and I just remember thinking, Oh, celebrities, feel free to play here. That's what's going on."

Jessica's gown for the Poiret: King of Fashion-themed gala was actually designed by Roberto Cavalli, who she walked the red carpet with.

Jessica was joined inside the party by John, who she had an on-off relationship with until their split later in 2007.

Vogue later apologized to Jessica in a statement to TODAY: "We are sorry that Jessica felt body-shamed by the anecdote in our Met piece.

"That was never our intent, but we understand her reaction and we apologize for including it."

The singer is now married to Eric Johnson and has three children with him: daughter Maxwell, eight, son Ace, six, and one-year-old daughter Birdie Mae.

Jessica was recently slammed by Wendy Williams for allowing Maxwell to read her memoir, in which she talks about being sexually abused by a family member at six years old.

She also detailed her years of addiction to alcohol and pills and trysts with ex John Mayer and other stars.

Wendy, a 55-year-old mother of one, said sharing all of this information with an eight-year-old was "not a good idea."

The talk show host said: "I understand being open with your kids, but that's a little too open. And the girl is a little too young for that."

Over the weekend, Jessica appeared in an Instagram video praising Maxwell: "Oh my, how in the world are you already reading so fast?"

Maxwell responded: "I'm on the second chapter of your book."

Jessica, appearing surprised, replied: "Are you? That's amazing. Well my book is hard to read."
