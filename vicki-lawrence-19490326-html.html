Vicki Ann Lawrence, sometimes referred to as Vicki Lawrence Schultz, is an American pop actress, comedian and singer best known for many of the characters she wrote on the CBS The Carol Burnett Show, where she appeared from 1967 to 1978, throughout the series. One of those characters is the Thelma Harper / Mama of "The Family", the cold, unaffected mother of a nervous and unlucky Eunice, even though Lawrence is 16 years younger than Burnett. Thelma Harper was the central character in the TV sitcom series Mama's Family on NBC and then, in the first episode. She also starred in Fox's sitcom series The Cool Kids. In 1973, she became a hit on the American charts with "The Night the Lights Went Out in Georgia". It reached number one on the charts of the United States and Canada. Lawrence has a number of Emmy Award nominations, including one in 1976. She has also been nominated for several Golden Globe Awards, all for The Carol Burnett Show.
