Sickening images show dead dog with its ribs showing through fur at squalid Ayrshire Ark kennels

 Posted on June 29, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  816 words  |  Martina Birk

HORRIFYING pictures of a dog lying dead on the filthy floor of a scandal-hit animal sanctuary have been revealed.

The mastiff-type mutt, with ribs visible through its fur, was surrounded by excrement and rubbish inside the makeshift home near Patna, Scotland.

Images also show other helpless pooches walking through piles of debris scattered around the Ayrshire Ark centre.

Our sickening snaps last night sparked calls for the kennels to be shut for good.

One source told The Scottish Sun on Sunday: “That poor creature was brought into a rescue centre to find a good home but instead it was just left in squalor.

“How sick to not even move its body. It’s horrifying.”

They added: “For a long time people have been worried about the dogs in the care of the Ayrshire Ark.

“Now it seems like their worst fears have been confirmed. We don’t believe this place should be allowed to continue operating.

“Somebody needs to go in and save those poor animals.”

Cops and the Scottish SPCA swooped on the derelict schoolhouse used by the organisation on Friday evening after we alerted welfare teams to the squalor inside.

Our harrowing images show stricken beasts left to live in their own faeces.

Mountains of clutter, including a broken cat carrier, old cardboard boxes and a bicycle, can be seen dumped in the corner of a room where two large dogs leap at a window, barking wildly.

Floors are littered with debris and machinery has been dumped at the spot where kids once hung their coats on pegs before lessons.

In a separate rundown classroom, a tiny terrier scarpers among shredded polythene and piles of poo.

Upturned food and water bowls are scattered across the rooms and there is no sign of any home comforts for the miserable mutts.

The horror comes after we revealed two hounds re-homed by the sanctuary in Patna attacked young kids hours after joining their new families.

And other dog lovers have reported their pets returning malnourished and sick from boarding in the kennels.

Our source added: “There have been numerous claims made in the past about animals being re-homed by the rescue covered in filth and urine burns.

“Some have been terribly underweight, some have been covered in wounds and others have displayed behavioural issues which have resulted in attacks on children.

“These dogs are neither happy nor healthy when they go to their new families.

“They are being put into environments which are completely unsuitable. Dogs who are known to be uncomfortable around children are being given to people with kids.”

The source also accused sanctuary bosses of losing pets in their care. They said: “We believe many dogs which have been in the hands of the rescue are now missing. Nobody can tell us where they are.

“We have also heard stories about people putting their dogs into the Ayrshire Ark for boarding and getting them back malnourished and poorly.

“People have always feared the conditions inside were bad, but I don’t think anyone will be prepared for just how awful it really is.”

Insiders claim that punters interested in re-homing dogs have regularly been denied access to the schoolhouse — sparking fears the rescue had something to conceal.

Instead, visitors are allegedly told to wait in the car park while mutts are brought outside to meet them.

Another source said: “The fact that nobody gets to see inside the building had already set alarm bells ringing.

“We have never heard of anyone getting to go in and look at all the dogs, like you would at other rescues. People are expected to choose one they like from pictures online, then it’s brought outside for them to see when they visit.

“If they decide to go ahead and adopt, it’s the same story — the dog gets led out to the car park for them to take away.

“Either that or a driver delivers it to its new home.”

Families who adopted dogs in the past say they were never vetted to ensure they could offer a loving and suitable home.

They also claim the sanctuary didn’t request references.

Bosses at Ayrshire Ark describe themselves as a non-profit organisation.

They use social media to appeal for public donations which they claim will help save dogs’ lives.

Some loyal supporters have signed up to make monthly contributions, with others offering gifts like food for the hounds instead. The Ayrshire Ark’s Facebook page had vanished yesterday.

A Scottish SPCA inspector said: “We recently attended an address in East Ayrshire and our investigations are ongoing at this time.”

Cops added: “We were called to assist the SSPCA following a report of a dog having died at a property in Patna, Ayrshire. Inquiries are ongoing.”
