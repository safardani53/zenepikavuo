His career was interrupted by increasing Nazi influence over Hungarian politics, and as a Jew he fled Hungary's increasingly harsh anti-Semitic rule in 1938. After performing a season of Russian ballet in London at the Royal Opera House, he found refuge in Switzerland, where he stayed during World War II. Banned from directing there, he made a living as a pianist. After the war, Solti was appointed music director of the Bavarian State Opera in Munich in 1946.
