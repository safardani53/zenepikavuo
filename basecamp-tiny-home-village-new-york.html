A NEW tiny home community has opened up with 11 cabins available to book from $150 a night.

Guests will love the modern tiny homes for their impressive amenities and stunning natural surroundings.

Basecamp is the first tiny home village in the Western New York ski destination of Ellicottville.

Opening in February 2023, the community boasts 11 cabins sleeping between two and six people each, with nightly rates starting at $150.

Each home has a modern kitchenette, a washer/dryer, a full bathroom, and between 280 and 800 square feet of living space.

The community is just a stone’s throw from the nearby ski slopes of Sky High Adventure Park and Holiday Valley Resort, meaning it is likely to get booked up during the winter.

But guests can also enjoy the serene natural surroundings in the summer - and the community claims to be perfect for wedding parties, bachelor and bachelorette parties, golf trips, and more.

The chief executive of the community, Bob Carbone, is hoping that it will lead to guests becoming more interested in tiny living.

“I mean this is a house, there is a washer and dryer it has a bathroom. it has a full kitchen,” he explained to WKBW.

“Basically, anything you can do in a house, you can do here.”

TESTING OUT TINY

If you’re thinking of downsizing, a tiny home vacation can be the perfect way to test out this lifestyle.

Tuxbury Tiny House Village is another community of tiny vacation homes in South Hampton, New Hampshire - around 45 miles north of Boston.

Guests have five unique houses to choose from, all with their own differing aesthetics and sizes.

Nightly stays range from $135 for the cheapest model to $145 for a 309 square feet home that sleeps six.

Guests will notice a luxury aesthetic throughout, with natural wood paneling and high-quality craftsmanship.

The five homes are circled around a communal fire pit, and the nearby Tuxbury Pond offers great fishing opportunities. 

You can also book unique tiny home stays on Airbnb.

For example, one couple has created a stunning stargazing dome in the middle of the Great Smoky Mountains for $268 a night.

But if you’ve decided that a tiny home is what you want full time - there are villages all over America where you can settle down small.
