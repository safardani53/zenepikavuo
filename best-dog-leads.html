A GOOD leash is an essential part of the kit for your dog and most owners will have a couple handy.

But with so many options around, how do you know which one is the best dog lead for your pet?

Well, to start with, consider your dog’s personality type.

For puppies and younger dogs, a training lead can be helpful for them to get used to being on a lead.

This is also true if it’s a new dog for you.

If your dog loves to explore - but isn’t so good at coming back when you call - a good retractable one is best.

But if your dog is well behaved, and you just need something to guide it when crossing the road for example, you can be a bit more flexible with the type of lead you pick.

So from training leads to leather ones, here are some of the best dog leads around.

Is a dog collar better than a harness?

This depends on your dog.

A collar is usually much more comfortable for your dog as it isn’t as restrictive.

It’s also simpler to use as your dog can just wear it all the time.

But for certain breeds, such as pugs, or if your dog likes to pull, harnesses might be more suitable as they don’t put as much pressure on their body.

The downside is of course that you do have to put it on and take it off for walks.

Your dog will also need to wear a collar featuring your name and address while out and about, even if it’s wearing a harness - this is a legal requirement in the UK.

What lead is best for a dog that pulls?

For a dog that pulls constantly, a harness might be better than a collar as this puts less pressure on your pet’s body if it suddenly runs off.

This should be used with a strong and non-retractable leash of around 1.5m.

This means that your dog will have some freedom to explore, but can be kept under control at all times.

How to stop dog pulling on lead

You can only stop your dog from pulling on its lead through training, and the RSPCA recommends doing this slowly and over time through rewards.

To start, you have to stand still and quiet, allowing your dog to have full freedom on its lead.

If it’s running off and ignoring you, you have to continue standing still. But the moment that it comes back to you, you can lavish it with attention and a treat.

While walking, if your dog starts running off, you have to stand still until they come back. And the moment that they do, you can reward them with a treat.

By repeating this over time, your dog will learn to walk with you on a lead and pay attention to your call.

What length of dog lead is best?

Different dog trainers have different preferences for the ideal length of a dog lead but anywhere between 1m and 2m can work.

For puppies and dogs that pull, a shorter lead is generally preferred.

If your dog is pretty obedient, a longer or retractable option can also work.

But of course, the length of the lead will also depend on how tall you and your dog are.
