As things were wrapping up, it was time for Mark to pitch what was coming up next.

"Next, we're gonna read our poll resorts, re-results," Mark sputtered.

XMAS DRAMA

Mark seemed absolutely furious during Tuesday's episode of Live.

Producer Michael Gelman and Mark's wife and co-host Kelly informed him of a yearly tradition that happens on the program.

It was brought to Mark's, attention that the Live crew had to wear holiday sweaters in the near future.

"We don't have to wear any holiday sweaters do we?," Mark asked Kelly, 53, at their talk show desk.

Michael, 62, interjected: "Oh yes, every segment," answering before Kelly could.

Kelly joked that she had forgotten that Mark "was the new guy."

Mark has only been co-hosting with Kelly since April 2023, when he took over for Kelly's former co-host Ryan Seacrest, 48.

Michael explained to the Riverdale actor that for every segment, the co-hosts would have to wear a different holiday sweater.

Mark shot him a glare, staring at him in disbelief.

His look toward Michael intensified when the producer told him he could wear a "silk shirt" beneath his sweater.

'MONSTER' MICHAEL

As of late, Mark and Michael have been engaging on Live.

Last month, Mark blasted Live producer Michael for being a monster on the morning show.

The commotion started on an episode aired on November 3 of Live when the two hosts talked about their favorite items to eat from a New York Deli. 

Michael mentioned he preferred to add salt and pepper to his bacon, egg, and cheese sandwich.

Mark clapped his hands and agreed: "Salt and pepper!"

Michael interrupted: "Ketchup."

Mark turned to Michael and said: "No ketchup. You're a monster!"

Michael jokingly admitted: "Oh yeah!"

Kelly's husband repeated: "No ketchup."

The talk show producer would not budge and added: "It needs it."

Mark initially paused before mis mind registered what Michael actually said.
