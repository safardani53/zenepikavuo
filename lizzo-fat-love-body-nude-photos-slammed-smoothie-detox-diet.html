LIZZO bared all in nude photos to clap back at online trolls who slammed the singer for promoting an “unhealthy” 10-day smoothie cleanse. 

The 32-year-old hitmaker penned a note to her critics that said “I’m still fat and I love my body” as her fans were alarmed by a recent detox process she documented on social media. 

Lizzo showed her nearly 10 million Instagram followers that her eating schedule has consisted of liquids and supplements for the past week and a half. 

She showed herself drinking green smoothies in the morning, “beauty juice" at night, vegan protein bars as a snack and ingesting various capsules in between while soaking her feet in “herb water.”

The Truth Hurts rapper explained that she chose to give the program a try after she “drank a lot and ate a lot of food that f**ked my stomach up,” while in Mexico last month. 

“In reality, November stressed me the f**k out. I drank a lot, I ate a lot of spicy things and things that f**ked my stomach up.

“And honestly I wanted to reverse it and get back to where I was,” she added.

After she recapped her diet cleanse on Monday, fans were outraged she promoted “toxic diet culture.”

One person tweeted: “Lizzo….girl. Why? It was inevitable. The industry is so violent towards fat women. Of course she was going to submit to toxic diet culture. It was only a matter of time.”

“Lizzo promoted a smoothie detox diet and people are all “leave her alone she’s not bothering anyone” but detoxes like those are really bad.

“Ashe really shouldn’t be promoting them especially when she has such a large platform, @lizzo ily but this isn’t good :(,” another fired off. 

Alongside an ‘I was rooting for you’ gif, another wrote: “Not Lizzo promoting a 10 day detox fad diet including detox "supplements" and foot pads on her Instagram."

While a fourth said: “I’m like super bummed about lizzo promoting “a detox cleanse” on instagram tbh. 

“It’s perfectly normal and fine to want to lose weight but that does NOT mean you should be promoting unhealthy ways to do it?”

After the mass backlash, the body-positive artist posted a series of naked shots of her figure and clapped back at haters with a stern message. 

She wrote: “I detoxed my body and I’m still fat. I love my body and I’m still fat. I’m beautiful and I’m still fat. 

“These things are not mutually exclusive. To the people who look to me, please do not starve yourselves. I did not starve myself.”

As she bared all while filming herself in the shower, she continued: “I fed myself greens and water and fruit and protein and sunlight. 

“You don’t have to do that to be beautiful or healthy. That was my way. You can do life your way. Remember, despite anything anyone says or does! 

“DO WHAT YOU WANT WITH YOUR BODY,” she concluded in the passionate message.

The Juice songstress got real with her fans about the process and also admitted that days four, five and six were the “hardest” to get through. 

“I was never super hungry, I think I just wanted to stress eat and do things that were like, kind of self-harming,” she confessed.
