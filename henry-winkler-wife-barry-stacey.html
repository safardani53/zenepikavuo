FOR OVER FIFTY years, Henry Winkler has established himself as a sought-after actor and comedian.

The beloved entertainer has been married to his wife for over 40 years.

Who is Henry Winkler married to?

Back in 1976, Henry Winkler portrayed Fonzie on Happy Days - a certified sex symbol of the time.

That year, he saw his future wife - Stacey Weitzman - at a store, noting her beauty from the get-go.

"She was wearing purple parachute pants and she had red hair and without her even saying a word, I thought, 'Woah, beautiful woman standing in front of me,'" Winkler told People in 2019.

He returned to the store the following week and invited Stacey out for a soda.

Weeks later, Stacey moved into Winkler's place with a then-four-year-old son, Jed, from her first marriage.

She and Winkler married in 1978, and together share two additional children: Zoe and Max.

Now, the two of them are grandparents.

"We enjoy grandchildren together. We enjoy going to the movies together," Winkler added of his relationship with Stacey.

Who is Henry Winkler?

Born on October 30, 1945, Henry Winkler is a native of New York City.

His parents came to America in the early 1940s, seeking refuge from the Nazi invasion of Germany.

Winkler grew up facing difficulties in school, and was later diagnosed with dyslexia in his adult life.

Following high school, he attended Emerson College before studying at the Yale School of Drama.

He earned his Master's degree in 1970 and was invited to join the Yale Repertory Theatre company.
