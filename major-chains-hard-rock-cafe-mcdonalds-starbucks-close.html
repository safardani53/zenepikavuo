Raft of major chains including Hard Rock Cafe, McDonald's &amp; Starbucks close doors for good in import

 Posted on April 30, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  221 words  |  Aldo Pusey

ANOTHER store has closed in a main Denver shopping center, joining several others that have shuttered in recent months.

The Corner Bakery Cafe closed in the Denver Pavilions shopping center on August 31.

Corner Bakery Cafe, a sandwich shop and bakery, had been in that location for 25 years, according to local NBC affiliate KUSA.

The cafe still operates several other locations in the area.

But it's far from the only major store to close in or near the Denver Pavilions this year.

Denver Pavilions is part of the larger 16th Street Mall, which spans more than a mile of storefronts downtown.

The Hard Rock Cafe, which had also been in Denver Pavilions for 25 years, closed in July.

On top of that, McDonald's and Starbucks also closed their 16th Street Mall locations this year.

At least four other restaurants in the Denver area have also shuttered this year.

In addition, beloved breakfast spot Waffle Brothers closed both of its locations.

They were reportedly seized by the city and county of Denver due to unpaid taxes.

Signs were posted at both locations on July 14.

The restaurant owed the state more than $27,000 in unpaid taxes, according to The Denver Post.

A Chase Bank branch is also slated to close its doors in Denver in November.
