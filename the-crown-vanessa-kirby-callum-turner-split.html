THE Crown star Vanessa Kirby and her Hollywood actor boyfriend Callum Turner have split after four years together, I can reveal.

Representatives for the actress, who won a Bafta for her role as a young Princess Margaret in the Netflix series, last night confirmed that the pair have separated.

And sources told how the couple grew apart after ­spending long periods away from each other due to filming ­commitments.

Vanessa spent months on set for her roles in Mission: Impossible — Fallout in 2018 and last year’s Fast & Furious: Hobbs & Shaw.

Callum, who stars in the ­latest film adaption of Jane Austen’s Emma, played Theseus Scamander in 2018’s Harry ­Potter sequel Fantastic Beasts: The Crimes Of Grindelwald.

He also recently played the lead role of soldier Shaun Emery in BBC1 conspiracy thriller The Capture.

One source told me: “Vanessa and Callum are both rising stars in the acting world and in the last few years they have been flooded with offers.

“It’s a really exciting time for them both.

“But as their careers have taken off, their relationship has suffered and they have gradually grown apart.

“It’s very sad as they were once so close.

“But last year they made the difficult decision to go their separate ways and they both think it’s for the best.

“It’s all very amicable — things just weren’t working out.”

Vanessa and Callum met in 2014 while starring together in post-war British comedy flick Queen & Country and went on to become an item in 2015.

But they managed to keep their romance quiet until 2017, when Vanessa was pictured kissing co-star Tom Cruise on the set of Mission: Impossible.

The smooch for the action movie looked so passionate there were whisperings she and Tom were dating — so Vanessa decided to silence the rumours by opening up about her two-year romance with Callum.

She revealed in a magazine interview months later: “The most disconcerting thing was the people I didn’t think would believe it did — and they were texting my boyfriend, asking him, ‘Are you OK?’”

She said Callum was “just completely wonderful”, adding: “He’s my best friend. I guess you can either choose to talk about it or not.

“If you talk about it, it becomes interesting to people, but if you don’t, then it’s not.”

Sadly, it seems there’s no longer anything to talk about.
