Robert Pattinson, Kristen Stewart Fought About Her Affair: Top 5 Stories

 Posted on April 22, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  366 words  |  Larita Shotwell

Robert Pattinson and Kristen Stewart fought about her affair before their breakup, Jay-Z says Beyonce is not pregnant and Taylor Swift sticks her tongue out at Justin Bieber and Selena Gomez: See Us Weekly's top 5 stories from Monday, May 20 in the roundup!

1. Robert Pattinson, Kristen Stewart Fought About Her Affair, "Moody" Behavior Before Breakup

Maybe he hasn't forgiven her after all. Before their maybe-temporary, maybe-permanent breakup late last week, Robert Pattinson and girlfriend Kristen Stewart fought bitterly for several days — with Stewart's headline-making July 2012 fling with Rupert Sanders coming up again and again. 

2. Jay-Z: Beyonce Is Not Pregnant

Blue Ivy will apparently be an only child for a little while longer. Beyonce's husband, Jay-Z, quietly addressed the latest, particularly vehement rumor that his wife of five years is pregnant with her second child — speaking via email with Hot 97, NYC's most pre-eminent hip hop radio station to deny the report.

3. Taylor Swift Sticks Tongue Out at Justin Bieber, Selena Gomez Kiss: GIF

Someone's got an opinion on Justin Bieber and Selena Gomez's romantic reunion: Taylor Swift! The 23-year-old singer songwriter sat right next to BFF Gomez, 20, during Sunday's May 19 Billboard Music Awards in Las Vegas, and watched as Bieber, 19, accepted two awards onstage (including a Milestone Award, where he was met with boos).

4. Video: Justin Bieber Booed at Billboard Music Awards

The good news for Justin Bieber at Sunday's Billboard Music Awards in Las Vegas: The teen pop phenom won the night's fan-voted Milestone Award. The bad news? Accepting the statue inside the MGM Grand Garden, the 19-year-old singer had to contend with boos from the audience.

5. Keanu Reeves Looks Bloated at Cannes Film Festival

Whoa! Keanu Reeves, 48, was spotted at Cannes looking quite different from the slim-hipped looker he was a decade ago. The actor, whose last hit movie was in 2003, which saw the release of both The Matrix Revolutions and Something's Gotta Give, is at the French film festival to promote his directorial debut, The Man of Tai Chi, but he didn't seem quite ready for the spotlight on Sunday, May 19.
